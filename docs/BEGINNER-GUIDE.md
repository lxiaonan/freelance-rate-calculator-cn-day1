# Beginner Guide | 小白使用文档

## 中文

### 这个项目适合谁

- 刚开始接单，不知道该怎么报时薪、日薪或项目价的人
- 已经在做自由职业，但报价经常靠感觉、怕报低也怕报高的人
- 想给不同客户准备多套报价方案的人

### 第一次打开，先做什么

1. 先点首页右上角的“加载示例”，不要一上来就填自己的真实数据。
2. 看顶部四个核心结果：建议时薪、项目报价、已保存方案数、风险提示。
3. 再从 Step 1 开始改数字，观察 Step 2、Step 3、Step 4 会怎么变化。

### 使用前要准备哪些信息

- 你希望自己每个月实际拿到多少收入
- 你每个月固定要支出的成本
- 你每周大概有多少小时真的能拿来计费
- 你一年准备休几周假
- 你的沟通、修改、管理等非计费时间大概占多少
- 如果你要估算某个项目，再准备项目工时、交付周期和风险系数

### 按这个顺序操作，最不容易出错

#### Step 1：建立报价基线

1. 在“目标月收入”里填你希望稳定达到的收入，而不是某一次理想暴击单的收入。
2. 在“每月固定成本”里把房租、社保、软件订阅、设备折旧这类长期成本算进去。
3. “每周可计费小时”不要填满 40 小时。对多数自由职业者来说，真实可卖时间通常比想象中少。
4. “非计费时间占比”建议诚实一点，因为沟通、开会、写方案、返工都要吃掉时间。
5. 如果你在估算单个项目，再补“预估项目工时”“交付周数”“修订轮次”和“项目额外风险系数”。

#### Step 2：看懂结果，不只看一个数字

1. 先看“建议时薪”，这是最基础的报价锚点。
2. 再看“建议日薪”和“项目报价”，判断它们是不是落在你目标客户能接受的区间。
3. 看“容量信号”和“风险提示”，如果系统一直提示风险高，通常不是工具坏了，而是你的工时、缓冲或成本设置过紧。

#### Step 3：保存多套方案

1. 你可以按客户类型各存一套，比如“创业团队”“中型公司”“长期合作客户”。
2. 每改完一套参数就点“保存方案”。
3. 到方案库里对比不同客户、不同工期、不同风险的差距，别只记一个价格。

#### Step 4：生成客户摘要

1. 页面会自动生成一份双语客户摘要。
2. 你可以先复制出来，再粘到微信、邮件、Notion 或正式报价单里。
3. 如果你要留痕或归档，就用“导出摘要”。

### 一个最简单的上手示例

1. 点“加载示例”。
2. 把目标月收入改成更接近你自己的数值。
3. 把每周可计费小时改成你真实能卖出去的时间。
4. 看建议时薪是否明显高于你现在的报价。
5. 如果高很多，优先检查是不是你过去低估了非计费时间，或者把项目修订成本算漏了。

### 新手最常见的三个误区

- 误区 1：把“每周可计费小时”当成“总工作时长”。
  实际上你每天还有沟通、整理、对账、找客户等时间，这些通常不能直接收费。
- 误区 2：只看项目总价，不看时薪是否健康。
  如果项目总价看起来还能接受，但换算后时薪很低，这个项目依然可能不划算。
- 误区 3：不保存不同场景。
  不同客户的沟通成本、风险和修订轮次差很多，最好保留多套方案。

### 数据会不会上传

- 不会。这个项目是纯前端页面，主要计算都在浏览器本地完成。
- 你保存的方案主要留在当前浏览器环境里。
- 如果你换浏览器、清缓存，之前保存的内容可能会消失，重要结果请及时导出。

### 最适合什么时候用

- 接新客户之前，先给自己建立一个底价基线
- 对方让你“随便报个价”时，快速算出一个不容易吃亏的范围
- 想把零散报价经验整理成可复用模板时

## English

### Who This Is For

- People starting freelance work and unsure how to price hourly, daily, or project-based work
- Freelancers who still quote by intuition and worry about undercharging or overcharging
- Anyone who wants separate pricing scenarios for different client types

### What To Do On Your First Visit

1. Click `Load Demo` first instead of typing your real numbers right away.
2. Look at the top outputs first: suggested hourly rate, project quote, saved scenarios, and risk hints.
3. Then change the Step 1 inputs and watch how Step 2, Step 3, and Step 4 react.

### What Information You Should Prepare

- The monthly income you want to actually take home
- Your fixed monthly costs
- The number of hours per week you can truly bill
- Vacation weeks per year
- Your non-billable overhead ratio
- If you want a project quote, prepare project hours, delivery timeline, and risk level too

### The Safest Way To Use It

#### Step 1: Build Your Pricing Baseline

1. Put your realistic target income into `Target monthly income`, not a once-in-a-while dream month.
2. Add real fixed costs such as rent, insurance, subscriptions, and equipment overhead.
3. Do not fill `Weekly billable hours` with your full working week. Real sellable time is usually much lower.
4. Be honest with `Overhead ratio` because communication, planning, revisions, and admin work all consume time.
5. If you are quoting one project, also fill project hours, timeline, revision rounds, and project risk.

#### Step 2: Read The Results Properly

1. Start with the suggested hourly rate because that is your core anchor.
2. Then review the daily rate and project quote to see whether they match the client type you are targeting.
3. Pay attention to the capacity and risk signals. If the tool keeps warning you, your setup is likely too tight.

#### Step 3: Save Multiple Scenarios

1. Create one scenario per client type, such as startup, mid-size company, or long-term retainer.
2. Click `Save Scenario` after each version.
3. Use the scenario library to compare the effect of risk, scope, and collaboration style.

#### Step 4: Generate The Client Summary

1. The app creates a bilingual client-facing summary automatically.
2. Copy it into email, chat, Notion, or a formal proposal.
3. Use export if you want to archive the result.

### A Simple First Practice Run

1. Click `Load Demo`.
2. Replace the income goal with something close to your own situation.
3. Change billable hours to the number you can truly sell.
4. Check whether the suggested hourly rate is much higher than what you currently charge.
5. If it is, the usual reason is that your previous pricing ignored overhead, revision cost, or risk.

### Three Common Beginner Mistakes

- Mistake 1: treating billable hours as total working hours.
  Admin work, meetings, sales, and revisions usually cannot all be billed directly.
- Mistake 2: only looking at total project price.
  A project can still be unhealthy if the implied hourly rate is too low.
- Mistake 3: keeping only one scenario.
  Different clients create very different revision, risk, and communication costs.

### Does It Upload My Data

- No. This is a static frontend tool and the calculations run locally in your browser.
- Saved scenarios mainly stay in the current browser environment.
- If you switch browsers or clear storage, local data may disappear, so export important results.

### Best Times To Use It

- Before replying to a new client
- When someone asks you to “just give a rough quote”
- When you want to turn scattered pricing experience into reusable templates
